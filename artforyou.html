Art For You

The past inspired art magazine.

Honoring the history of art and design through typesetting.

Project Overview

Create a magazine that combines elements from historic eras, as well as influences from contemporary designers and artists, with the aim of honoring past design legends.

Services

Print Design & Typesetting

Software

Challenge

Design a magazine that is attractive, provides information, and is influenced by historical design.

Solution

Create a design and typesetting style that is inspired by research findings from various sources.

Conceptual Planning

Introduce the idea behind “Art For You” magazine and its focus on celebrating the past of art and design, with a specific emphasis on the historic eras. While outlining the goals and objectives of the magazine, including educating readers about the significance of historic eras, showcasing contemporary design work, and inspiring a love and appreciation of art and design.

Into the Past

The design of the magazine was inspired by the rich history of art and design, and our aim was to honor the designers and artists who have made significant contributions to the field. I wanted the magazine to reflect the evolution of design, from traditional techniques to contemporary practices, and to demonstrate how the past has shaped and influenced the present.

Research

Start by researching the Bauhaus era and its impact on art and design. Look at the work of famous Bauhaus designers and artists, as well as contemporary designers who have been influenced by the movement. Use this research to gather ideas for the overall design and content of the magazine.

Concept

Define the Concept: Based on the research, define the concept for the magazine. The goal is to create a publication that celebrates the past of art and design, with a focus on the Bauhaus era, and encourages readers to appreciate its significance.

Audience

Define the Target Audience: Consider who the magazine is for. Is it for design enthusiasts, artists, or art students? Understanding the target audience will help to determine the tone, content, and design of the magazine.

Content

Determine the Content: Based on the concept and target audience, determine the content for each issue. This might include features on Bauhaus artists and designers, interviews with contemporary designers, articles on design history, and showcases of current design work.

Layout

Design the Layout: Using the information gathered from the research and content planning, start to design the layout of the magazine. The design should be visually appealing, inspired by the Bauhaus era, and have a focus on typography.

Typesetting

Typesetting: Determine the typesetting style for the magazine. This should be inspired by the Bauhaus era and be consistent with the overall design of the publication.

Revisions & Production

Review and Production: Review the design and content, making any necessary revisions to ensure that the magazine is visually appealing, informative, and aligned with the concept and target audience. Once the design and content have been finalized, it's time to start production. This might involve printing the magazine, publishing it online, or both.

Thankful Teachings

Creating a magazine that combines elements of eras history and design, with a focus on typography, as a tribute to the past of art and design. The magazine will serve as a reminder of the significance of this style and encourage readers to learn and appreciate its history.
